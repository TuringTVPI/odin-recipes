Fjordfire Salmon All recipes

The Chronicle

Fresh from the icy fjords and kissed by flame, this salmon is a prize worthy of any longhouse table. Bright lemon, fragrant dill, and a generous brush of butter turn a humble fish into a warrior’s reward. Best served before anyone starts boasting about the size of the one that got away.

The Provisions

  • salmon fillets
  • butter
  • garlic
  • fresh dill
  • lemon
  • sea salt
  • cracked black pepper
  • virgin olive oil
  • baby potatoes
  • green beans
  • honey
  • mustard

The Ritual

  1. Wake the flames. Stoke the hearth until the fire burns steady and bright, fierce enough to sear but wise enough not to turn your prize into ash.
  2. Prepare the catch. Lay the salmon upon an iron tray, then brush with melted butter, garlic, lemon juice, honey, mustard, sea salt, and cracked black pepper.
  3. Scatter the green herbs. Crown the fish with fresh dill and slices of lemon, for even warriors deserve a touch of elegance between raids.
  4. Gather the sides. Toss the potatoes with oil and salt, then roast them near the fire until golden. Steam or char the green beans until tender.
  5. Offer it to the flame. Cook the salmon over steady heat until the flesh flakes easily beneath your fork and the top glows like sunset over the fjord.
  6. Guard the treasure. Do not overcook it. A dry salmon brings shame upon the hall and muttering from the clan.
  7. Serve with honour. Plate the salmon with potatoes and green beans, spoon over any buttery juices, and raise a horn to the fisher who returned with dinner.
