The Chronicle

When the winter winds bite, nothing restores a weary warrior quite like a steaming cauldron of hearty stew. Slow-simmered beef, root vegetables, and a generous splash of ale combine to create a meal strong enough to satisfy even the fiercest berserker.

The Provisions

  • stewing beef
  • onions
  • carrots
  • potatoes
  • garlic
  • celery
  • mushrooms
  • beef stock
  • dark ale
  • tomato purée
  • thyme
  • bay leaves
  • virgin olive oil
  • sea salt
  • cracked black pepper

The Ritual

  1. Light the cauldron. Stoke the fire until the iron pot grows hot enough to welcome the feast.
  2. Brown the spoils. Sear the beef in a little oil until every side is richly browned. Remove and set aside while the battle continues.
  3. Prepare the harvest. Slice the onions, carrots, celery, mushrooms, and potatoes into hearty chunks fit for hungry warriors.
  4. Summon the broth. Return the beef to the pot with the vegetables. Pour in the ale and beef stock, then stir through the tomato purée, thyme, bay leaves, sea salt, and cracked black pepper.
  5. Let patience triumph. Hang the cauldron over a gentle fire and allow the stew to simmer slowly until the beef yields to even the dullest blade and the broth grows rich and thick.
  6. Taste the victory. Remove the bay leaves and season once more if the Allfather demands it.
  7. Gather the shield wall. Ladle generous portions into sturdy bowls, tear fresh bread for sharing, and raise a horn of mead before the first spoonful. No warrior should face the feast alone.
