Jarl's Roast LambAll recipes

Description

Celebrate a successful raid with this slow-roasted leg of lamb. Seasoned with fragrant herbs, served with roasted root vegetables.

Provisions

  • lamb (whole or leg)
  • garlic
  • rosemary
  • thyme
  • virgin olive oil
  • sea salt
  • pepper
  • potatoes
  • carrots
  • parsnips
  • onions

Ritual

  1. Stoke the hearth. Build a strong fire until the flames settle into a bed of glowing embers worthy of the feast to come.
  2. Prepare the offering. Score the lamb with your knife and press slivers of garlic into the cuts. Rub generously with oil, rosemary, thyme, sea salt, and cracked pepper.
  3. Gather the harvest. Quarter the onions, peel the carrots and parsnips, and toss them with the potatoes, a little oil, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Lay the feast upon the fire. Arrange the vegetables beneath the lamb in a sturdy roasting tray or iron pot, allowing the juices to drip down as it cooks.
  5. Patience. Roast slowly over the steady heat, turning the vegetables and basting the lamb with its own rich juices until the meat is tender and the skin is beautifully browned.
  6. Grant the beast its rest. Remove the lamb from the fire and leave it untouched for a short while. Even the fiercest warrior deserves a moment’s peace before the carving begins.
  7. Gather your clan. Carve generous portions, serve with the roasted vegetables, and raise a horn of mead to honour the Allfather before the first bite.
