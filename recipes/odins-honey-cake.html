Odin's Honey Cake

The Chronicle

Sweetened with golden honey and rich enough to silence even the loudest feast hall, this cake is a worthy end to any Viking banquet. Soft, spiced, and crowned with walnuts, it is best served warm while someone nearby dramatically claims they could have eaten two.

The Provisions

  • plain flour
  • baking powder
  • ground cinnamon
  • ground ginger
  • sea salt
  • unsalted butter
  • honey
  • brown sugar
  • eggs
  • milk
  • vanilla extract
  • walnuts
  • double cream

The Ritual

  1. Summon the warmth. Stoke the hearth until the oven chamber is ready for a cake worthy of the Allfather.
  2. Prepare the dry hoard. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, ginger, and a pinch of sea salt.
  3. Melt the golden tribute. Warm the butter, honey, and brown sugar together until smooth and glossy.
  4. Bind the offering. Stir the honeyed mixture into the dry ingredients, then add the eggs, milk, and vanilla until the batter is smooth.
  5. Crown it with walnuts. Fold through a generous handful, saving a few for the top so the hall knows you were not stingy.
  6. Commit it to the fire. Pour the batter into a prepared cake tin and bake until risen, golden, and a skewer comes out clean.
  7. Honour the table. Let the cake cool slightly, drizzle with extra honey, and serve with double cream before the clan starts circling like ravens.
